Michael L. Iuzzolino

Senior Research Scientist at Meta

I'm a senior research scientist at Meta, currently focused on multimodal large language models for recommendation systems. My work spans foundation model pretraining, reinforcement learning-based post-training (GRPO/DAPO), chain-of-thought distillation, and novel architectures that decouple reasoning from retrieval in LLMs. I'm deeply AI-native, building automated experimental pipelines and agentic workflows to accelerate research iteration.


Previously at Meta, I led projects in Reality Labs on contextual AI for next-generation wearable interfaces — designing ultra-low-friction user experiences powered by multimodal goal inference, and building the data collection, annotation, model training, and deployment pipelines to ship them.


I received my Ph.D. and M.S. in Computer Science from the University of Colorado Boulder, advised by Michael C. Mozer. Prior to Meta, I worked with Samy Bengio at Google Brain and with the Applied Science Group at Microsoft Research. I completed a triple major B.S. at the University of Arizona in applied mathematics, neuroscience & cognitive science, and molecular & cellular biology.


I've traveled 39+ countries, which included working on farms, couch surfing, mountaineering, taking care of 200 sled dogs in northern Finland, and long-distance solo trekking in the Andes, the Himalayas, Patagonia, etc. I'm also a lifelong musician — after a decade-long hiatus, I'm back to playing guitar on a collection of Caparison, Strandberg, and Fender Strats.
