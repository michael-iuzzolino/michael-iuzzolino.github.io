Travel

Christmas Markets and Thermal Baths in Budapest, 2025

Budapest, Hungary

Five days in Budapest with 20,000-30,000 steps daily and not a single Uber or bus ride. We caught a violin concert at Matthias Church in Buda Castle (the best violinist I've ever seen live), gorged on chimney cakes and Christmas market food, and explored the crypts beneath Buda Castle where Dracula was supposedly imprisoned. The highlight was a late-night session at the Turkish thermal baths from 10pm to 1am — the saunas and hot springs alone are reason enough to come back. We capped it off with a night cruise along the Danube with live music and glittering views of the city. Definitely coming back.

Ending a Month in Europe — Zurich, 2025

Zurich, Switzerland

We wrapped up a month-long European adventure in Zurich — a fitting finale to a whirlwind trip through the Netherlands, Austria, and Hungary. The city is stunning, meticulously clean, and surrounded by alpine scenery that makes you want to lace up hiking boots immediately. A beautiful way to close out the trip before heading home.

Mozart, Schnitzel, and Spontaneity — Vienna, 2025

Vienna, Austria

We randomly decided to hop on a plane to Vienna after a week in the Netherlands, and it turned out to be one of the best spontaneous decisions of the trip. Vienna is stunning — imperial architecture at every turn, incredible coffee culture, and Mozart's preserved apartment right in the heart of the city. We visited his home, wandered through grand palaces, and ate our weight in schnitzel and Sachertorte. The train from Vienna to Budapest was arguably nicer than Gran Class on the Tokyo-Sendai Shinkansen.

Amsterdam and Haarlem, 2025

Netherlands

Fresh off a month in Korea, we headed to the Netherlands to kick off a European adventure. Amsterdam delivered its signature charm — canals, bikes, and cozy brown cafes — but the real standout was Haarlem, a smaller city nearby that quickly became a favorite. We spent afternoons working from Dutch coffee shops, comparing every latte to what we'd just experienced in Seoul (a tough act to follow), and gearing up for an unplanned sprint through Vienna, Budapest, and Zurich.

Busan and Late Nights in Korea, 2025

Busan, South Korea

After two weeks in Seoul, we took the KTX to Busan — naturally watching Train to Busan on the way. Busan was incredible: late-night fish markets, seaside temples, cliffside foot-spa cafes with ocean views, and a 24/7 self-serve ramen shop we stumbled into at 3am in a random beach town. We hiked up a massive hillside at 1am to find a cafe to work from, explored mountain villages, and demolished ungodly amounts of bingsu. We'd fully flipped our schedule by this point — waking at 5pm and sleeping at 8am — and discovered PC Bangs where you can game for $5 and order katsu curry delivered straight to your desk. By the end of the month, we'd walked roughly 240 miles across Seoul and Busan.

Two Weeks Exploring Seoul, 2025

Seoul, South Korea

Two weeks in Seoul with 10+ miles of walking every single day and eating absolutely everything in sight. We wandered Insa-dong for salt butter bread, stumbled into a souffle pancake cafe, hiked the Naksan Trail along the old city wall, climbed Namsan Mountain to N Seoul Tower, explored Olympic Park, circled Seokchon Lake, visited a wonderfully weird art museum, and found "Rain Report" — a rain-themed cafe with a rooftop sprinkler system, weather-report menus, and a spiced pepper foam latte that turned out to be one of the best I've ever had. Seoul's cafe scene alone deserves its own post. We hit 33,000 steps in a single day, ate the best pizza since Hong Kong at a place called "No More Pizza" (half Hawaiian, half shrimp-corn-potato chip), and spent way too many hours in PC Bangs. Seoul is easily one of my favorite cities in the world.

Mt. Rainier — Skyline Trail Loop, 2025

Mt. Rainier, Washington

After almost 8 years of living in Washington, Dieu My and I finally made it to Mt. Rainier. We completed the Skyline Trail Loop — about 1,450 ft of elevation gain over 5.5 miles, much of it through snow, and all in Keen sandals with no socks. The mountain is breathtaking, the wildflower meadows are surreal, and we had a cozy cabin with a private jacuzzi to recover in after the long hikes. These mountain roads are begging for the 911 next time.

Remote PhD in Taiwan, 2020

Taipei, Taiwan

Dieu My and I arrived in Taipei on January 1st, 2020. We spent 1 month living in the Songshan district near Taipei Arena. We walked 10+ miles through the city nearly everyday and were able to check out most of Taipei's districts. Unlike in the USA, you can walk anywhere at anytime of the day and be safe! We also took the high speed rail down to Kaoshiung City on the southern side of the island for a weekend — beautiful place with amazing hotels that will blow your mind. Night markets are a must; hiking is incredible; Louisa Coffee has some of the best coffee I've ever had. There's an endless list of praises I have for Taiwan and it is easily one of my favorite countries.

Remote PhD in Japan, 2020

Osaka, Japan

After spending January in Taipei, Dieu My and I spent February in Osaka, Japan. We stayed in a really nice apartment just 5 minutes walk from Dotonburi. In the same way that Taiwanese hotels will make you forever detest American hotels, Japanese bathrooms will make you detest American bathrooms and think of them as barbaric relics of the past. You have to experience it yourself to understand.


Unfortunately, I spent a good amount of time in Japan sick. I had to go to the doctor twice as an uninsured foreigner, yet it was the most pleasant, painless, and cheapest medical experience of my life. When I wasn't sick, we were walking all over Osaka, working at coffee shops, and gorging on sushi, udon, takoyaki, and okonomiyaki.
